## Definition
Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) are specialized neural networks designed to operate on graph-structured data (nodes and edges). They are excellent for modeling relationships and dependencies.

## How It Applies Here
Nguyen Thanh Minh Duy has researched and applied GNNs, including Open-Set Recognition with Multi-Objective GNN, for intrusion detection in CAN bus networks.
